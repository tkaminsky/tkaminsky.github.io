## Introduction

Curriculum Learning seeks to improve the sample efficiency or performance of some learned model by training it on specially-selected subsets of a dataset, which slowly increase in complexity over the course of training, rather than training it on all of the data at once. This is more like how people learn---instead of being randomly taught math theorems drawn out of a hat, we follow a well-defined _curriculum_, starting with simple tasks like counting on our fingers, then learning about abstractions like counts, numbers, variables, functions, and so on. Only after we master one stage of the curriculum can we move on to the next, more complex step in the subject.

The main functions of curriculum learning are to __denoise__ and __guide__ a learning process. When optimizing over a function with multiple local optima, either because of complexity/multimodality or noisy data, a curriculum serves to smooth out this curve by giving the model an initial subset that is 'easier'---lower noise and more informative.

### Training Objective

Consider a training process that trains some model ***M*** on data $\mathcal{D}$ $\mathbb{R}$. The survey authors define curriculum learning (CL) as a system implementing the following two components:

* **Difficulty Measurer**: A module which determines the relative difficulty of data samples $d \in $ ***D***.
* **Training Scheduler**: A module which decides when a model should begin training on data samples of a given difficulty. 

To avoid forgetting over the training process, the data that a scheduler feeds into a model is typically increasing, so $D_1 \subset D_2 \subset \dots \subset D_T = \mathcal{D}$. Thus, by the end of the curriculum, the model should be trained on the complete task.

### Varieties of CL

The authors describe four varieties of CL: [predefined CL](#predefined-cl), [self-paced learning](#self-paced-learning), [transfer-teacher](#transfer-teacher), and [RL Teacher](rl-teacher). 

The first requires you to designate both the difficulty measurer and the training schedule explicitly. The second and third automate the difficuly measuerer, but still require you to choose the training scheduler. Only the last method fully automates the learning pipeline, making it especially desirable, though potentially more complex.
