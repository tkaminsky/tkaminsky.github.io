Hello! I'm Thomas Kaminsky, a senior studying stats and math at Harvard.

I'm originally from a small town in Washington State called [Walla Walla]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walla_Walla,_Washington"), known for [Whitman College]("https://www.whitman.edu/"), its local liberal arts school, its agriculture, especially as a thriving wine region and the home of our world-famous [Walla Walla Sweet Onions]("https://www.sweetonions.org/") (the Washington State vegetable since 2006!), and its incredible outdoor recreation. 

Eventually, I moved to Michigan to spend a year at Interlochen Arts Academy, where I could focus full time on [playing the flute]("https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CnO_wST6QxM") and preparing for college auditions. Here I had an incredible time, being able to play some really wonderful music, participate in masterclasses with incredible musicians including Sir James Galway, Mark Sparks, and Amy Porter, and develop as a musician and person.

Now, I am beginning my senior year at Harvard University, pursuing a joint concentration in statistics and mathematics, with a secondary in computer science. In my spare time, I play music, participate in the arts scene at Harvard, and love hiking, camping and the outdoors.

At the moment, I am working on research projects with Professor Karthik Desingh at the University of Minnesota's Robotics: Perception and Manipulation (RPM) Lab and with Professors Dieter Fox and Joshua R. Smith at the Robotics and State Estimation Lab (RSE-Lab), working on the second year of the Amazon Picking Challenge.

Thank you so much for visiting my website!
