Recent Projects
Optimal Experimental Design for Linear Models						              Aug–Dec 2022
Wrote a research paper examining optimal experimental design, a general method for creating low-variance estimates of regression features in linear and generalized linear models while labeling only a small subset of our training data. This is the classical analog of the active learning setting, which aims to improve sample efficiency by interactively querying a user for only the most informative ground truth labels during training.

Now You See Me: Segmenting Unknown Object Instances For Warehouse Grasping		              June–Aug 2022
Presented work from my ten-week REU program at the University of Washington. I applied recent breakthrough methods from Fox lab to detect and track unknown object instances over time in warehouse bin scenes. The resulting system was evaluated by Amazon in August 2022, outperforming their established baselines. Models were trained using PyTorch, synthetic data was generated with NViSII, a recent Python-based renderer from NVIDIA, and the perception system was integrated with the robot and depth camera using ROS.

Comparing Feature Representations for Adversarial Robustness					  Jan–May 2022
Wrote a research paper as a final project assessing the impact of feature transformations on adversarial robustness against an untargeted projected gradient descent attack. Models implemented using PyTorch, and attacks using FoolBox.
